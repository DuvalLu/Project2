Small Breeds

Information and Attributes of Small Breed Dogs Small breed dogs are generally defined by the American Kennel Club (AKC) as weighing less than 22 pounds and/or standing shorter than 16 inches at the shoulder The Smart Canine. They can be further categorized into toy breeds (often under 7 lbs) and small breeds (up to 22 lbs). Many are well-suited for apartment living due to their lower exercise needs, but some still require outdoor activity American Kennel Club. Common Traits Across Small Breeds Size & Weight: Typically 4–22 lbs; toy breeds often 4–7 lbs The Smart Canine+1. Height: Usually under 16 inches; some toy breeds under 10 inches The Smart Canine. Coat Types: Short, long, curly, silky, or hairless (e.g., American Hairless Terrier) American Kennel Club+1. Temperament: Varies widely — from playful and affectionate to alert and independent. Many are intelligent and adaptable DogChart. Exercise Needs: Generally low to moderate; some terriers and hounds need more activity American Kennel Club. Barking Tendency: Often more vocal than large breeds, especially when alert Best Life. Training: Trainability varies; some are quick learners, others may be stubborn Best Life. For apartment living: Many small breeds adapt well; avoid overly boisterous or high-energy types if space is limited American Kennel Club. For low-maintenance: Consider hairless or low-shedding breeds like the American Hairless Terrier or Poodle The Smart Canine. For alertness: Breeds like Alaskan Klee Kai or Brussels Griffon are good watchdogs American Kennel Club. Tip: Always research breed-specific needs, as even within the “small” category, traits and care requirements can differ greatly.
